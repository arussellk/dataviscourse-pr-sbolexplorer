SBOLExplorer - The Synthetic Biology Open Language Explorer

SBOLExplorer is a visualization of synthetic genetic components using the Synthetic Biology Open Language (SBOL). SBOL defines a data format that represents synthetic genetic parts that researchers can use as DNA building blocks to create genetic constructs or larger synthetic genetic components. SBOLExplorer visualizes this dataset of synthetic genetic parts as well as the inheritance relationships between them.

All of the above are glyphs that represent synthetic genetic parts. Each synthetic genetic part has a glyph assigned to it by the SBOL standard. The nodes in our tree represent genetic parts so each node has its own corresponding glyph.
For example: the full data for the glyph titled "BBa_15610" is found here.


The rectangular bar under each node's glyph is a visualization of the genetic part's DNA sequence, it's colored according to where the children nodes’ DNA sequences exist in the parent node’s DNA sequence.
Any white space indicates areas where the DNA codons in the DNA sequence are not any defined genetic part and when the DNA sequences of children overlap in the parent sequence, the colors overlap.
The edges are correspondingly color coded to show which children comprise which part.
For example: The genetic part BBa_B0015 is comprised of 2 children genetic parts (B0010 and BBa_B0012), and B0010 is in the first portion of BBa_B0015's DNA sequence and BBa_B0012 is in the latter portion of BBa_B0015's DNA sequence.


Each search result has a rectangular bar that shows the result's relevance, or it’s PageRank, scaled comparatively to the other results. This relevance is assigned by the SynBioHub database of genetic parts.

Data

Our data is sourced from SynBioHub. Through SynBioHub, users can view data about a genetic component by visiting its reference page (BBa_I5610 example reference). Notice that SynBioHub does not have a way to view the hierachical nature of a genetic component tree. SBOLExplorer was created to fill this need.
